Nature & Eco Tourism

Parks & Rivers

Not every natural wonder in Ilocos Sur requires a long hike. The province is also home to protected monuments, scenic river systems, and eco-parks that offer a more relaxed way to experience nature. From a World War II battlefield turned natural monument, to a city aquarium perfect for kids, there is something here for every kind of visitor.

Protected Areas & Eco Parks

4 Parks & Rivers to Explore

Park 01

Bessang Pass Natural Monument

Historical & Ecological Site

Bessang Pass is both a natural monument and a WWII historical site. In 1945, Filipino and American forces broke through the heavily fortified Japanese line here, one of the most significant battles in the liberation of the Philippines. A memorial park and monument now stand at the pass, surrounded by lush mountain scenery. It is a rare place where history and nature exist side by side.

Park 02

Abra River

Major River System

The Abra River is one of the longest and most important river systems in northern Luzon, flowing through both Ilocos Sur and the neighboring province of Abra. For centuries it has sustained local communities through fishing and farming. Today it also offers opportunities for kayaking, riverside picnics, and scenic views, especially beautiful during the golden hour.

Park 03

Candon City Aquarium & Park

Family-Friendly · Wildlife

Located in Candon City in the southern part of Ilocos Sur, this aquarium and park features local freshwater fish species, native plants, and small animals. It is one of the most family-friendly nature attractions in the province, a great stop if you are traveling with children or want a relaxed outdoor experience without a long hike.

Park 04

Kankantuban Eco Park

Nature & Culture Combined

Kankantuban Eco Park is a green space that celebrates both the natural environment and the cultural identity of Ilocos Sur. It features native flora, walking trails, and exhibits about the province's ecology and indigenous plants. A peaceful, educational destination that is often overlooked but well worth a visit.

🏳️ Fun Fact — Bessang Pass & the Liberation of Luzon

General Yamashita, the Japanese commander nicknamed "The Tiger of Malaya," used Bessang Pass as part of his final defensive line in northern Luzon. Filipino soldiers of the Philippine Commonwealth Army fought for months to break through, suffering heavy losses before finally succeeding in May 1945. The victory effectively opened the road to the liberation of the entire Ilocos region. The soldiers who died there are honored at the monument to this day.
