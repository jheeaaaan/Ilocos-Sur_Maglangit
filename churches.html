Historic Churches

The churches of Ilocos Sur are among the oldest and most beautiful in the Philippines. Built by Spanish missionaries during the colonial era, these stone churches have withstood earthquakes, wars, and the test of time. Many are still active parishes today places of living faith, not just history. When visiting, please dress modestly and be respectful of ongoing religious services.

Colonial-Era Churches

Churches of Ilocos Sur

Church 01

Santa Maria Church

UNESCO Baroque Church

The only one of the four UNESCO-listed Philippine Baroque Churches built on a hilltop. To reach it, you climb a wide brick stairway and the view from the top is breathtaking. Built in the 18th century, its thick stone walls and defensive layout give it a fortress-like appearance unlike any other church in the country.

Church 02

St. Paul Metropolitan Cathedral

Vigan Cathedral · 16th Century

Also known as the Vigan Cathedral, this is the main cathedral of the Diocese of Vigan. It faces the famous Plaza Salcedo and has been the center of Catholic life in Vigan since the Spanish colonial period. The cathedral has undergone several renovations over the centuries but retains its grand colonial facade.

Church 03

Bantay Church

Adjacent to the Bell Tower

One of the oldest churches in the Philippines, Bantay Church dates back to 1591. It stands right beside the famous Bantay Bell Tower, and together they form one of the most iconic heritage sites in Ilocos Sur. The church is known for its thick stone walls and classic Ilocano baroque architecture.

Church 04

St. Francis Xavier Parish

Colonial Parish Church

Dedicated to St. Francis Xavier, one of the founding figures of the Jesuit order, this parish church serves a local community with deep colonial roots. It features traditional Ilocano church architecture and remains an active place of worship for its parishioners, giving visitors a genuine glimpse of faith still alive in Ilocos Sur.

Church 05

Santa Catalina Church

Quiet Parish · Colonial Roots

A peaceful and less-visited parish with deep colonial roots, Santa Catalina Church offers a quieter, more intimate experience compared to the busier churches in Vigan. It is a hidden gem for visitors who want to appreciate Ilocos Sur's religious heritage away from the tourist crowds.

⛪ Visitor Reminder

All of these churches are active parishes, Mass is held regularly. Please dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees), speak quietly inside, and avoid visiting during Mass if you are just sightseeing. Most churches are open to visitors free of charge.
