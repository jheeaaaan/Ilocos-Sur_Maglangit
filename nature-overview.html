Nature & Eco Tourism

Nature & Eco Tourism

Most people visit Ilocos Sur for Vigan's cobblestone streets — but the province has a completely different side that many tourists never get to see. Behind the colonial facades lies a landscape of cascading waterfalls, rugged mountain trails, ancient rivers, and protected forests that have been standing long before the Spanish ever arrived. This category is for those who want to go beyond the heritage zone and explore the natural heart of Ilocos Sur.

Highlands

Mountains of Ilocos Sur

The eastern side of Ilocos Sur borders the Cordillera mountain ranges, giving the province a variety of peaks for hikers of all skill levels. These mountains are not just scenic, many carry deep historical significance. Tirad Pass on Tirad Peak is where the young General Gregorio del Pilar made his heroic last stand against American forces in 1899, buying time for Emilio Aguinaldo to escape.

The mountains of Ilocos Sur are generally cooler than the lowlands, especially at higher elevations. Hiking season is best from November to May during the dry months. Always hire a local guide, the trails are not always well-marked, and guides know the terrain, weather patterns, and local wildlife.

Tirad Peak
Site of the 1899 Battle of Tirad Pass. General Gregorio del Pilar, only 24 years old, died defending this mountain pass.
Mt. Cainiao
A popular trekking destination with rewarding views of the Ilocos Sur lowlands and the South China Sea in the distance.
Mt. Tuwato
A less-explored mountain known for its pine forests and cool climate, ideal for campers and nature photographers.
Mt. Namandiraan
One of the least crowded hiking spots in the province, perfect for those who want a peaceful mountain experience.
⚔️ Fun Fact — The Boy General of Tirad Pass

General Gregorio del Pilar was only 24 years old when he died at Tirad Pass in December 1899. He led just 60 Filipino soldiers against 500 American troops to buy time for President Emilio Aguinaldo's escape. American soldiers, impressed by his bravery, reportedly gave him a full military burial with honors. His last diary entry read: "There is no greater glory than to die for one's native land."

Cascades

Waterfalls of Ilocos Sur

Ilocos Sur's waterfalls are some of the most underrated natural attractions in northern Luzon. Hidden deep in the mountains and forests, many of them require a hike to reach, but that is exactly what makes them so rewarding. You often have the entire waterfall to yourself, with no vendors, no crowds, just the sound of rushing water and the cool mist on your face.

The largest and most accessible is Pinsal Falls in Santa Maria, which has facilities and picnic areas nearby. More adventurous visitors seek out hidden gems like Baey Anito Falls, whose name in Ilocano loosely translates to "spirit place", so named because locals believe spirits dwell near its waters.

Pinsal Falls
The largest waterfall in Ilocos Sur. Located in Santa Maria with picnic facilities. Great for families and first-time visitors.
Aw-asen Falls
One of the tallest waterfalls in the Ilocos Region. Multi-tiered and surrounded by dense forest, best reached with a local guide.
Dawara Falls
A scenic waterfall tucked in the interior of Ilocos Sur, relatively unknown to mainstream tourism and wonderfully unspoiled.
Kinmarin Falls
Known for its crystal-clear pool at the base, perfect for a refreshing swim after the hike through the forest trail.
Baey Anito
Literally means "spirit place" in Ilocano. Locals believe the area is inhabited by nature spirits, adding a mystical atmosphere to an already stunning waterfall.
🌿 Fun Fact — The "Spirit Place" Waterfall

Baey Anito Falls gets its name from the Ilocano word anito, which refers to ancestral spirits or nature deities in pre-colonial Filipino belief. Before Christianity arrived, the Ilocano people believed that powerful spirits called anito lived in rivers, trees, and waterfalls. Offerings were made to them before entering the forest. Even today, some locals still leave small offerings near the falls out of respect, just in case. 🌀

Rivers & Parks

Parks & Rivers

Beyond the mountains and waterfalls, Ilocos Sur also has protected natural areas, scenic river systems, and eco-parks that showcase the province's biodiversity. The Abra River, one of the longest rivers in northern Luzon, flows through the province and has sustained Ilocano communities for thousands of years through fishing and farming.

For those who prefer a more structured nature experience, Candon City Aquarium & Park and Kankantuban Eco Park offer curated encounters with local wildlife and plant life, great for families with children or visitors who want nature without the long hike.

Bessang Pass
A natural monument and WWII battle site. In 1945, Filipino and American forces broke through the Japanese line here — one of the war's most significant Philippine victories.
Abra River
One of the longest river systems in northern Luzon. It flows through Ilocos Sur and Abra province, supporting agriculture, fishing, and local livelihoods.
Candon Park
The Candon City Aquarium & Park features local freshwater species and native plants. A good stop for families visiting the southern part of Ilocos Sur.
Kankantuban
An eco park that celebrates the natural and cultural environment of Ilocos Sur. Features native flora, walking trails, and educational exhibits.
🏳️ Fun Fact — Bessang Pass & World War II

The Bessang Pass battle in 1945 is considered one of the most strategically important Filipino victories of World War II. Japanese General Yamashita had fortified the mountain pass as part of his last defensive line in northern Luzon. Filipino guerrillas and soldiers, fighting alongside American forces, finally broke through after months of brutal combat, helping seal the liberation of the Philippines. Today a monument stands at the pass to honor those who fought there.
