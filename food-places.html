Best Food Places

From a historic cafe on the most iconic street in Vigan to a homestyle kitchen that cooks like your Ilocano lola, these five places represent the best of what Ilocos Sur's food scene has to offer. All of them serve authentic Ilocano dishes, all of them are affordable, and all of them are worth making time for.

Restaurants & Eateries

5 Places to Eat

Place 01

Cafe Leona

Heritage Cafe · Calle Crisologo

Possibly the most famous restaurant in Vigan. Cafe Leona sits right at the corner of Calle Crisologo in a beautifully preserved ancestral house the kind of place where the ambience alone is worth visiting for. The menu features classic Ilocano dishes done well: longganisa, bagnet, empanada, and a house special Vigan rice that will make you want to order a second serving.

Place 02

Hidden Garden

Garden Restaurant · Nature Setting

True to its name, Hidden Garden is a restaurant built inside a lush garden setting with native huts, coconut trees, and greenery all around your table. The experience of eating here feels completely different from a regular restaurant peaceful, open-air, and beautifully Ilocano. The menu covers a wide range of Filipino and Ilocano dishes, and the portions are generous. Great for groups and family celebrations.

Place 03

Irene's Vigan Empanada

Street Food Stall · Freshly Fried

Not a restaurant, a stall. But one of the most beloved food stops in all of Vigan. Irene's is famous for making empanadas fresh to order, right in front of you, in a large wok of hot oil. You watch the dough go in, wait about two minutes, and walk away with a hot, crackling, golden empanada for around ₱35. It is one of the most authentically Vigan food experiences you can have.

Place 04

Lampong's Restaurant

Local Favorite · Generous Servings

A no-frills, much-loved local restaurant that focuses on doing Ilocano food right. Lampong's is known for its generous servings, affordable prices, and consistent quality. The bagnet here is regularly praised as some of the best in Vigan, crispy, well-seasoned, and served with a huge mound of rice. A favorite with locals and travelers who know to eat where the locals eat.

Place 05

Kusina Felicitas

Homestyle Ilocano Cooking

Kusina Felicitas is the kind of restaurant that feels like eating at a warm Ilocano family's home because it essentially is. The cooking here is homestyle, authentic, and deeply comforting. The pinakbet, dinengdeng, and poqui-poqui are made the traditional way, with real bagoong and garden-fresh vegetables. If you want to understand what everyday Ilocano cooking actually tastes like, this is the place.

💰 Budget Guide to Eating in Ilocos Sur

Street food (empanada, longganisa): ₱20–₱45 per piece. | Carinderia meal (rice + viand + soup): ₱80–₱120. | Mid-range restaurant (Cafe Leona, Lampong's): ₱150–₱350 per person.| Hidden Garden (group meals): ₱200–₱450 per person. You can eat extremely well in Ilocos Sur on a very tight budget.
