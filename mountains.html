Mountains of Ilocos Sur

Ilocos Sur is not just flatlands and coastlines. Its eastern edge rises into the foothills of the Cordillera mountain ranges, giving hikers and adventurers a variety of peaks to explore. Some carry the weight of history, like the famous battle at Tirad Pass, while others are simply quiet, beautiful mountains waiting to be climbed. Whatever your reason for going up, the view from the top is always worth it.

Peaks & Trails

4 Mountains to Climb

Mountain 01

Tirad Peak

Historic Hiking Destination

The most historically significant mountain in Ilocos Sur. On December 2, 1899, 24-year-old General Gregorio del Pilar and his 60 soldiers made their heroic last stand at Tirad Pass against 500 American troops, buying time for President Aguinaldo to escape. Del Pilar died on this mountain, and a national shrine now marks the spot. Hikers come not just for the trail but to walk where history was made.

Mountain 02

Mount Cainiao

Ideal for Trekking

Mount Cainiao is one of the most popular trekking destinations in Ilocos Sur. The trail offers a moderate challenge suitable for beginners and experienced hikers alike. At the summit, on a clear day, you can see the rolling lowlands of Ilocos Sur all the way to the distant glimmer of the South China Sea, one of the best views in the province.

Mountain 03

Mt. Tuwato

Pine Forests & Cool Climate

Mt. Tuwato is known for its cool climate and stretches of pine forest that cover the upper slopes. It is a less-explored peak compared to Tirad and Cainiao, which makes it appealing to hikers who want a quieter, more immersive nature experience. The pine-scented air and the cooler temperatures at elevation make it feel like a completely different world from the hot Ilocos lowlands below.

Mountain 04

Mount Namandiraan

Less Crowded Hiking Spot

If you want a mountain all to yourself, Namandiraan is the one. It is one of the least visited peaks in Ilocos Sur, meaning the trails are quiet, the environment is undisturbed, and the experience feels genuinely off the beaten path. The name Namandiraan in Ilocano relates to the idea of "dwelling" or "staying", and once you reach the top and see the view, you will understand why.

🥾 Hiking Tips for Ilocos Sur Mountains

Always start hikes early in the morning, afternoons can bring sudden rain, especially from June to October. Wear proper trail shoes, bring at least 1.5 liters of water per person, and pack light snacks for energy. For Tirad Peak specifically, register at the local tourism office before heading up as it is a protected national shrine. Best season to hike: November to April.
