Activities & Adventure Tourism

Adventure Activities

Ilocos Sur rewards travelers who go beyond the heritage sites and get involved. From the clatter of hooves on cobblestone to the spin of a potter's wheel, from the pull of a river current to the open road on two wheels through ancient barrios — these four activities represent the most memorable and hands-on ways to experience the province. None of them require special skills. All of them will give you a story to tell when you get home.

Things to Do

4 Adventure Activities

Activity 01

Kalesa Ride in Vigan City

Cultural · Heritage Experience

The kalesa — a two-wheeled horse-drawn carriage — is the most iconic way to move through the cobblestone streets of Vigan's UNESCO Heritage Zone. These carriages have been part of Ilocano life since the Spanish colonial era and remain fully operational today, carrying tourists through Calle Crisologo and past Mestizo District ancestral houses. A typical tour covers the main heritage streets and takes about 30 to 45 minutes. Negotiate the price with the cochero (driver) before you board. Morning rides, when the streets are quieter and the light is golden, offer the most magical experience.

Activity 02

Pottery Making at Pagburnayan Jar Factory

Hands-On Craft · 400-Year Tradition

Located along Liberation Boulevard in Vigan, Pagburnayan (from the Ilocano word burnay, meaning earthen jar) is a working pottery village where artisans have been producing traditional burnay jars using the ancient foot-kick wheel technique for over 400 years. Visitors are welcome to watch the potters at work — and many are invited to try shaping clay on the wheel themselves. The jars produced here are fired in wood-burning kilns and are prized for their durability and distinctive earthy glaze. It is one of the few places in the Philippines where this centuries-old craft tradition is still practiced daily. Admission is free; some workshops charge a small fee to try the wheel.

Activity 03

River Kayaking Activities

Outdoor Adventure · Beginner-Friendly

For travelers who want to experience Ilocos Sur's natural landscape from the water, river kayaking is available along select rivers in the province. Paddling through calm river sections offers a completely different view of the Ilocos countryside — rice fields, bamboo groves, and limestone formations visible from water level. The activity is suited to beginners and does not require prior kayaking experience. Life vests and basic instruction are provided by local operators. Best done during the dry season (November to April) when water levels are stable and the current is gentle.

Activity 04

Cycling Tours Around Heritage Areas

Scenic · Self-Guided or Guided

Vigan and the surrounding heritage towns of Ilocos Sur are exceptionally well-suited for cycling. The streets within the UNESCO Heritage Zone are narrow and low-traffic, and the flat terrain of the Ilocos plains makes longer rides very manageable even for casual cyclists. Several operators and guesthouses in Vigan offer bicycle rentals by the hour or by the day. A popular route covers Calle Crisologo, the Mestizo District, Vigan Cathedral, and continues out to the tobacco fields and barrios on the city's outskirts. The best cycling hours are early morning (6–9 AM) when streets are cool and traffic-free.

🗓️ Planning Your Activities Day

Most of these activities can be combined into a single action-packed day. Start with a sunrise cycling tour through the heritage zone, followed by a visit to Pagburnayan mid-morning. After lunch, take a kalesa ride through Calle Crisologo, then head out for an afternoon kayaking session before ending the day with a sunset at the beach. Wear comfortable clothes, bring sunscreen and a water bottle, and wear closed shoes for pottery and cycling activities.
