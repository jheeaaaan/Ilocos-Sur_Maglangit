Waterfalls of Ilocos Sur

Tucked behind the mountains and deep inside the forests of Ilocos Sur are some of the most beautiful, and most overlooked, waterfalls in northern Luzon. Many of them require a hike to reach, but that is exactly what makes the experience so rewarding. No vendors, no crowds, just the sound of rushing water, cool mist, and nature completely untouched.

Hidden Cascades

5 Waterfalls to Discover

Falls 01

Pinsal Falls

Largest Waterfall in Ilocos Sur

Located in the municipality of Santa Maria, Pinsal Falls is the largest and most accessible waterfall in the entire province. It has a wide, dramatic cascade that drops into a natural swimming pool at its base. The site has picnic huts, restrooms, and an entrance fee, making it a great destination for families and first-time nature visitors in Ilocos Sur.

Falls 02

Aw-asen Falls

One of the Tallest in the Region

Aw-asen Falls is one of the tallest waterfalls in the entire Ilocos Region, cascading down in multiple tiers through dense forest. The hike to reach it passes through scenic trails and small streams. Because it is farther from the highway, it remains largely undiscovered, meaning you will almost certainly have it entirely to yourself.

Falls 03

Dawara Falls

Off the Beaten Path

Dawara Falls is a hidden gem tucked deep in the interior of Ilocos Sur. It remains virtually unknown to mainstream tourism, which means the trail, the water, and the surrounding forest are completely unspoiled. If you want a waterfall experience that feels like a true discovery rather than a tourist attraction, Dawara is it.

Falls 04

Kinmarin Falls

Crystal Clear Swimming Pool

Kinmarin Falls is best known for the beautiful crystal-clear pool that forms at its base, calm, clean, and incredibly refreshing after the forest hike. The water is said to be so clear you can see straight to the bottom even in the deepest parts of the pool. A favorite among locals for weekend swimming trips.

Falls 05

Baey Anito Falls

The Spirit Place

The most mystical waterfall on this list. The name Baey Anito comes from the Ilocano word anito, ancestral spirits or nature deities worshipped by the Ilocano people before the Spanish arrived. Locals believe nature spirits dwell near the falls, and some still leave small offerings at the water's edge out of respect. The atmosphere here is unlike any other waterfall in the province.

💡 Before You Visit Any Waterfall

Always check the weather before heading out, waterfalls can rise and become dangerous very quickly during or after rain. Bring waterproof sandals or trail shoes, enough drinking water, and snacks for the hike. For remote falls like Dawara, Kinmarin, and Baey Anito, always go with a local guide, trails are not always marked and can be slippery. Best visiting months: November to April (dry season).
