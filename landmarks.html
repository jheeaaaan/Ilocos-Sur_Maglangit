Cultural & Heritage Tourism

Heritage Landmarks

Ilocos Sur is home to some of the most well-preserved historical landmarks in the Philippines. These are not just old buildings they are living pieces of history that tell the story of a province shaped by centuries of culture, colonization, and resilience. From the cobblestone streets of Vigan to the red soil of the Ilocos countryside, every corner has a story to tell.

Tourist Spots

Must-Visit Landmarks

Spot 01

Vigan City

UNESCO Heritage City

Vigan is the capital of Ilocos Sur and one of the best-preserved Spanish colonial cities in Asia. Its streets, churches, and ancestral homes have stood for over 400 years. In 1999, it was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site, a rare honor that recognizes Vigan as a treasure of all humanity, not just the Philippines.

Spot 02

Calle Crisologo

Famous Cobblestone Street

The most iconic street in Vigan. Calle Crisologo is a narrow cobblestone road lined with centuries-old ancestral homes. Cars are not allowed here, only kalesa (horse-drawn carriages) can pass. At night, oil lamps light the street, making it feel like you have stepped back into the 1800s.

Spot 03

Plaza Salcedo

Historic Plaza · Fountain Show

Located at the heart of Vigan, Plaza Salcedo is named after Spanish conquistador Juan de Salcedo who founded Vigan. The plaza is best known for its spectacular dancing fountain show every evening, where water jets move in sync with music and colorful lights ,completely free to watch!

Spot 04

Syquia Mansion

Former Presidential Residence

The Syquia Mansion is the ancestral home of President Elpidio Quirino, the sixth President of the Philippines who was born in Vigan. The mansion is now a museum where visitors can see the original furniture, personal photographs, and family heirlooms of the Quirino family exactly as they were left.

Spot 05

Bantay Bell Tower

Historic Watchtower · 16th Century

Built in 1591, the Bantay Bell Tower is a freestanding watchtower that stands beside the Bantay Church. During the Spanish colonial era, it was used to watch for pirates and signal warnings to the townspeople. Today it offers one of the best panoramic views of Vigan and is one of the most photographed spots in Ilocos Sur.

Spot 06

Red Soil of Ilocos Sur

Natural Landscape Feature

One of the most visually striking features of Ilocos Sur is its bright red soil, commonly seen along roadsides and hillsides of the province. The red color comes from iron oxide in the earth. This distinctive soil has been used for centuries to make the famous burnay pottery, the iconic clay jars that Ilocos Sur is known for all over the Philippines.

💡 Travel Tip — Best Way to See the Landmarks

The best way to explore Vigan's landmarks is by renting a kalesa (horse-drawn carriage) for around ₱150–₱200. A short ride will take you past Calle Crisologo, Plaza Salcedo, and other key spots. For Plaza Salcedo's dancing fountain show, arrive at around 6:30 PM, it is free and runs every evening!
