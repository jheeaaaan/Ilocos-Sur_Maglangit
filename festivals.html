Festivals & Cultural Events

Major Festivals

Five festivals. Five very different reasons to visit Ilocos Sur. Whether you are drawn to street dancing, lantern displays, food competitions, or a province-wide cultural fair — there is a festival here that will be the highlight of your trip.

Celebrations to Attend

5 Major Festivals

Festival 01

Viva Vigan Binatbatan Festival

April–May · Street Dancing · Weaving Arts

The biggest and most colorful festival in Ilocos Sur. Held every April or May in Vigan City, Binatbatan celebrates the ancient tradition of abel iloko weaving through elaborate street performances where dancers recreate the motions of weaving using colorful costumes and props. The streets fill with music, giant parade floats, trade fairs, and cultural competitions. If you can only attend one festival in Ilocos Sur, make it this one.

Festival 02

Longganisa Festival

Food Festival · Vigan's Iconic Sausage

A festival entirely dedicated to the most famous food product of Vigan the garlicky, savory Vigan Longganisa. The Longganisa Festival features cooking competitions, eating contests, giant longganisa displays, and plenty of free samples. Local manufacturers compete for the title of best longganisa, and visitors get to be the judges. A delicious, fun, and very Ilocano kind of celebration.

Festival 03

Sinait Garlic Festival

Agricultural Festival · Sinait

Held in the municipality of Sinait, one of the biggest garlic-producing areas in Ilocos Sur this festival celebrates the humble but mighty Ilocos garlic that flavors almost every dish in the province. Expect garlic-braiding competitions, garlic cooking contests, farm tours, and stalls selling every garlic product imaginable. A surprisingly fun event that shows how central this single ingredient is to Ilocano culture and livelihood.

Festival 04

Kannawidan Ylocos Festival

February · Province-Wide Cultural Fair

The Kannawidan Ylocos Festival is a month-long celebration held every February that showcases the best of everything Ilocos Sur has to offer heritage, food, crafts, agriculture, and performing arts. The word kannawidan means "tradition" in Ilocano. The festival features trade fairs, cultural shows, sports competitions, and exhibits from all 32 municipalities of the province, making it the most comprehensive celebration of Ilocano identity in the entire year.

Festival 05

Raniag Twilight Festival

December · Festival of Lights · Vigan

Raniag means "to glow" or "to light up" in Ilocano, and that is exactly what this December festival does to Vigan. The heritage streets, ancestral homes, and plazas are adorned with thousands of lanterns, lights, and illuminated displays. The cobblestone streets of Calle Crisologo, already atmospheric at night, become absolutely magical during Raniag. It is one of the most beautiful things you can witness in the Philippines.

🎊 Fun Fact — Kannawidan Means "Tradition"

The word Kannawidan comes from the Ilocano root word kawid, meaning to hold on to, to preserve, or to keep. The full phrase kannawidan translates roughly to "that which is kept" or "our traditions." The festival was established to ensure that Ilocano culture, crafts, and agricultural practices are not forgotten as the province modernizes. Every year, all 32 municipalities of Ilocos Sur participate — bringing their local products, performances, and traditions to one massive provincial celebration.
