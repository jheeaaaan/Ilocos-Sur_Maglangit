Ilocano Local Dishes

These are the six dishes you absolutely must eat in Ilocos Sur. Not suggestions must. Each one is deeply rooted in Ilocano culture and history, and each one tastes noticeably better here than anywhere else in the Philippines because of the local ingredients: Ilocos garlic, Ilocos bagoong, Ilocos vinegar, and locally raised pork that has been feeding Ilocano families for generations.

Must-Eat Dishes

6 Dishes to Try

Dish 01

Vigan Longganisa

Iconic Ilocos Sausage

The most famous food product of Vigan and probably the most well-known Ilocano dish in the entire Philippines. Unlike the sweet longganisa from other regions, Vigan Longganisa is intensely garlicky, savory, and slightly sour a flavor profile that is uniquely Ilocano. Traditionally served for breakfast with garlic fried rice and a fried egg. You can also buy it raw to cook at home as pasalubong.

Dish 02

Bagnet

Crispy Deep-Fried Pork Belly

Pork belly that has been boiled, dried, and then deep-fried twice until the skin becomes impossibly crispy almost like pork crackling. It is the Ilocano version of lechon kawali, but many will argue it is better. Served with fresh tomatoes, onions, and bagoong on the side. The crunch when you bite into a good piece of bagnet is one of the great pleasures of Ilocano dining.

Dish 03

Vigan Empanada

Crispy Street Food Pastry

Vigan Empanada is unlike any other empanada in the Philippines. The shell is made from orange rice flour that turns golden and shatteringly crispy when deep-fried. Inside is a filling of Vigan longganisa, raw egg, and grated green papaya. Sold by street vendors all over Vigan for around ₱30–₱45, always made fresh to order, always best eaten immediately while the shell is still crackling.

Dish 04

Pinakbet

The Ilocano Vegetable Dish

One of the most famous Filipino dishes and it originated right here in Ilocos. Pinakbet is a mix of vegetables (bitter melon, eggplant, squash, okra, string beans, tomatoes) cooked with pork and seasoned with bagoong. The fermented paste is what makes the dish it gives the vegetables a deep, savory, slightly funky flavor that no other seasoning can replicate. A true taste of Ilocano home cooking.

Dish 05

Dinengdeng

Light Ilocano Vegetable Soup

If pinakbet is the bold Ilocano vegetable dish, dinengdeng is its lighter, brothier cousin. It is a simple soup of vegetables malunggay, squash blossoms, string beans, bitter melon cooked in a broth flavored with bugguong (fermented fish). The result is surprisingly clean and flavorful despite the minimal ingredients. A staple of everyday Ilocano home cooking that is healthy, comforting, and deeply satisfying.

Dish 06

Poqui-Poqui

Ilocano Roasted Eggplant Dish

The most underrated dish on this list and possibly the one that will surprise you most. Poqui-Poqui is made from roasted eggplant mixed with scrambled egg, onions, garlic, and tomatoes. The roasting gives the eggplant a smoky, silky flavor that transforms what could be a boring vegetable dish into something genuinely delicious. Best eaten with steaming hot white rice and a side of bagoong.

🛒 Where to Find These Dishes

All six dishes are available at local carinderias and restaurants throughout Vigan and Ilocos Sur. For street food (longganisa, empanada), head to Calle Crisologo or the area near Plaza Burgos in the evening. For sit-down meals, Cafe Leona and Kusina Felicitas serve excellent versions of all these dishes. Budget tip: a full meal at a local carinderia costs only ₱80–₱120 including rice and soup.
