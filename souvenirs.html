Shopping & Souvenir Tourism

Souvenirs & Shopping

The best souvenirs from Ilocos Sur are not mass-produced trinkets, they are handcrafted objects with centuries of tradition behind them.

From hand-thrown burnay jars to hand-woven abel Iloko textiles, from carved wooden crafts to dried local delicacies wrapped in native packaging, what you bring home from Ilocos Sur carries the story of the people who made it.

Most of these items are available along Calle Crisologo and the surrounding shops in Vigan City, as well as directly from the craftsmen at their workshops.

What to Bring Home

5 Must-Buy Souvenirs

Souvenir 01

Burnay Jars

Traditional Craft · Vigan Exclusive

Burnay jars are the most iconic craft product of Ilocos Sur, hand-thrown earthen pottery that has been produced in Vigan using the foot-kick wheel method for over 400 years. The jars are fired in traditional wood-burning kilns, giving them their distinctive dark, earthy glaze and exceptional durability.

Originally used to store sukang Iloko (Ilocano vinegar), bagoong (fermented shrimp paste), and basi (sugarcane wine), burnay jars today are sought after as decorative pieces, garden ornaments, and collector's items.

The best place to buy directly from the source is Pagburnayan Jar Factory along Liberation Boulevard in Vigan.

Souvenir 02

Abel Iloko Textiles

Hand-Woven · Pre-Colonial Craft

Abel Iloko is the traditional hand-woven fabric of the Ilocos Region, produced on wooden looms using cotton threads in bold, geometric patterns and rich earthy colors.

The weaving tradition dates back to the pre-colonial era and has been recognized as a significant cultural heritage craft by the Philippine government.

Abel Iloko textiles are available as table runners, blankets, scarves, bags, and ready-to-wear clothing.

The fabric is known for its durability, distinctive texture, and striking visual patterns no two pieces are exactly alike. Look for authentic abel at the Crisologo Street shops and at the Abel Weaving Centers in Vigan and Pinili.

Souvenir 03

Wooden Crafts

Artisan Woodwork · Colonial Heritage

Ilocos Sur has a long tradition of woodcarving, a craft closely tied to the province's Spanish colonial heritage, the same artisan families that once carved the retablos (altar screens) and wooden saints for the region's churches continue the tradition today in workshops around Vigan.

Common wooden craft items include miniature kalesa carvings, religious figurines, decorative frames, furniture pieces, and small household items carved from local hardwoods. Many are available along Calle Crisologo and at the Vigan City Plaza shops.

Prices range from very affordable for small pieces to higher for large, intricately carved works.

Souvenir 04

Local Delicacies

Edible Souvenirs · Pasalubong

Ilocos Sur produces some of the most distinctive and beloved regional food products in the Philippines, many of which travel well and make excellent take-home gifts.

Top items to bring home include bagnet (deep-fried crispy pork belly, vacuum-sealed for travel), sukang Iloko (Ilocano cane vinegar in traditional burnay jars), longganisa (cured Vigan sausages), tupig (grilled rice cakes wrapped in banana leaves), and Vigan empanada mix.

Many of these items are available at the Vigan Public Market and along Calle Crisologo pasalubong shops.

Souvenir 05

Handmade Accessories

Artisan · Community-Made

A range of handcrafted accessories made from local materials are available throughout Ilocos Sur's souvenir shops. These include items made from abel Iloko fabric, native shells, local seeds, and recycled materials, all crafted by local artisans and community cooperatives.

Popular items include abel Iloko coin purses and bags, shell jewelry sets, seed bead necklaces and bracelets, woven hair accessories, and small woven baskets.

Many are made by women's cooperatives in and around Vigan, making your purchase a direct contribution to local livelihoods. They are lightweight, affordable, and distinctively Ilocano, perfect for bringing home as gifts for multiple people.

🛍️ Where to Shop in Ilocos Sur

The best souvenir shopping in Ilocos Sur is concentrated in Vigan City. Calle Crisologo and the surrounding streets of the heritage zone are lined with souvenir shops selling all of the above. For the most authentic and fairly-priced items, buy directly from the craftsmen, visit Pagburnayan for burnay, the abel weaving centers for textiles, and the Vigan Public Market for food delicacies.

Avoid buying from shops immediately in front of major tourist spots, as prices there tend to be inflated. Always ask if items are locally made, genuine Ilocos crafts are worth paying a fair price for.
